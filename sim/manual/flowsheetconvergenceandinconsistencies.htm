Flowsheet Convergence and Inconsistencies

 

Sim42 uses specified tolerance for both convergence and inconsistency checking. For the latter this is just the absolute error divided by a scale factor associated with that type of variable. If the returned value is bigger than the tolerance for that unit operation (normally inherited from the parent unit operation), an inconsistency error is raised.

The same calculation is used for recycle convergence tolerances and the iterations continue until the largest error is smaller than the flowsheet tolerance AND there are no stacked inconsistencies.  During solver iterations, consistency errors are stacked and discarded and will only make their effect known by preventing convergence. Unlike other simulators, a converged solution will not have inconsistencies.
Controller errors are handled similarly.
